Privacy Policy

The Wanting Compass · Last updated: July 20, 2026

The Wanting Compass ("the app") is built to work entirely on your device. This policy explains, plainly, what that means for your data.

What we collect

Nothing leaves your device. The app has no user accounts, no sign-in, and no server that it talks to. Specifically:

  • Your name (if you enter one) is stored only in the app's local storage on your device, so it can greet you and personalize your results. It is never transmitted anywhere.
  • Your age-gate response (whether you've indicated you're of legal drinking age where you live) is a simple on/off setting stored locally. It is self-reported, not verified, and used only to filter which results the app can show you.
  • Your quiz answers and results, and your selected language, are stored locally on your device (using Apple's standard UserDefaults storage) so the app can remember your last result without making you retake the quiz. This data stays on your device and is not synced, backed up to any server we control, or shared.

What we don't do

  • We don't use analytics or tracking SDKs.
  • We don't show ads or use ad networks.
  • We don't collect your location, contacts, photos, or camera access.
  • We don't sell or share any data with third parties, because we don't collect any data that could be shared.
  • We don't use cookies (the app isn't a website).

Children's privacy

The Wanting Compass is not directed at children. Some optional content within the app references alcohol in a lighthearted context, and the app carries an appropriate age rating on the App Store as a result. We do not knowingly collect any information from children, and as noted above, we don't collect personal information from anyone, regardless of age.

Data deletion

Because everything is stored locally, you're always in control: deleting the app from your device permanently removes all data associated with it. There is no account to delete, because there is no account.

Changes to this policy

If the app's data practices ever change — for example, if a future version adds an optional feature that syncs data — this page will be updated first, and the "Last updated" date above will reflect that change.
